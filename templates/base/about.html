About Render Farm

A render farm is an interconnected computing cluster that utilizes High Performance Computing (HPC) to create computer-generated imagery (CGI), typically used for visual effects in the entertainment industry. Since parallel computing allows each frame or tile to be rendered individually, distributed rendering makes it possible to produce large quantities of CGI in a relatively short period of time.
Rendering high-resolution CGI takes a lot of processing power and can become a bottleneck. It may take hours to render a single image, or even months to render a feature-length animated film. Render farms can help offload or accelerate such tasks, freeing up valuable computer resources for much more productive and creative work.

How It Works

A render farm is a huge collection of machines harnessed together to do one thing--render your 3D frames. But how do such computers render months worth of data in just hours. Well, they use nodes.Node is another word for a computer that is part of a host of computers whose sole purpose in life is to render. These nodes are controlled by a render management software--think of this program as the render overlord that tells all the nodes what to do. Each individual node can be instructed to render an individual frame, allowing multiple frames to be rendered at the same time.
